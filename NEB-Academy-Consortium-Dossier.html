01

What the call actually funds

Regional skills infrastructure and lifelong learning (CPD + VET) for the sustainable transformation of the lived environment — the built, natural and social spaces people inhabit — carrying the New European Bauhaus values of sustainability, inclusivity and aesthetics, with a strong tilt toward Architecture.

Non-negotiables

  • ≥ 2 target groups & ≥ 2 learning formats
  • One mandatory Systemic Transformation path · ≥48h + competency assessment
  • ≥ 15% of budget led by creative practitioners
  • Financial sustainability: ~€475k revenue ambition by 2028 (20% shared via FSM)

KPI load to carry

  • 390 graduates from EIT-labelled programmes
  • 730 successful professional-course participants
  • 770 learners with new entrepreneurial skills
  • Info sessions 24 Jun & 10 Jul · PIC registration needed early

07

Four subjects — each taught to several groups

The call separates what is taught (the subject) from how (the format) and who for (the target group). One subject reaches several groups through different formats — that's the "Skills Infrastructure" logic, and it avoids the content duplication the call penalises. Four subjects cover all four target groups.

Mandatory spine · the integrating path

Systemic transformation of the lived environment

The subject: leading and facilitating multi-actor NEB change across the frictions of design, finance, regulation and community. It's the call's one compulsory path (≥48h + competency assessment) and the place where the other three subjects meet on a real project. Maps straight onto urban-leadership and governance expertise.

Reaches TG1 TG2 TG3 TG4
48h path + masterclass TG3 #4 · systemic capacity TG1 #4 · co-creation with cities TG4 #3 · community stewardship

Circular, bio-based & regenerative building practices

The subject: the technical "how things are made" core — circularity, bio-based materials, timber, regenerative methods, plus the digital strand. This is built-environment sustainability in practice.

Reaches TG1 TG2 TG3 TG4
TG1 #2 · teach it TG2 #2 · do it on site
MOOC + micro-credentials (the 4 categories) · XR / lab when partner joins

Beauty in the built environment & city design

The subject: experiential quality of streets, façades, materials and public space treated as a design parameter — not decoration. NEB frames beauty as integral, so this is a built-environment competency, not a soft add-on.

Reaches TG1 TG2 TG3 TG4
TG1 #3 · beauty competency TG2 #6 · aesthetic literacy TG4 #1 · creatives
Co-creation workshops + masterclass · anchors the ≥15% creative earmark

Green financing & circular business models

The subject: EU taxonomy, green finance, regulatory de-risking and circular revenue models — the economic enabler that lets the other three scale and survive past the grant. Ricard's economics lead.

Reaches TG1 TG2 TG3 TG4
TG3 #1 · financial literacy TG2 #5 · circular models CCSI02 KPI
Transformation practices + policy lab · honestly TG2 / TG3-centred

How these reach everyone

Same subject, different doorways. Each subject above runs as a MOOC for broad uptake (TG4), a micro-credential for teachers (TG1), a workshop or XR lab for practitioners (TG2), and a policy lab for officials (TG3) — the two-track structure the call asks for.

Mass-uptake track: MOOC + micro-credentials
Transformation track: labs · workshops · the 48h path

Honest note: green financing genuinely centres on TG2 + TG3 — the faded badges aren't an oversight. Forcing a civil-society stream into it just to fill the grid reads worse to an evaluator than clean, real coverage.
